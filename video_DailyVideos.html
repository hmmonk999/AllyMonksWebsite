Camp Ozark: Daily Videos


Everyday at camp, the video team creates 2 minute long video that showcases 3-5 seconds clips of that day's events. The video team works together to film clips of campers doing various activies thoughout the day. As a video editor for the daily videos, I had half an hour to watch all the clips and piece together an entertaining video to show campers after dinner.

My Team

  • Me and 3 Other Videographers
  • 1 Video Editors
  • 1.5 hours editing

Time Taken

  • 6-7 hours filming
  • 1.5 hours editing

The Technology

  • Adobe Premiere Pro
  • Ronin S, Ronin M
  • Canon 80D
  • Drone
  • Wide Angle, Super Telephoto, and Super Telephoto Lenses

The Process

The rest of the video team and I spent the entire day filming and editing the Daily videos. We had 1-2 hours to look through all the film we captured and create a 2-minute video that highlighted that days events to show to campers. These types of videos had 4 sections that were 30 seconds long:

  • Section 1: Team Competitions - every day the campers had teams sports to play in the morning
  • Section 2: Morning Activites - campers could also sign up for different classes or to play on the ropes course or zipline
  • Section 3: Mishmash - a free-for-all where campers could go do any activity on the camp grounds
  • Section 4: Special Event - at the end of camp everyday there was a big themed party for the campers to attend



When we were editing this video, we had preset music that we would time when we put our transitions in. We wanted our transitions to show off a lot of motion and be timed to the music.



To make sure that we captured all of the actions, especially when campers could be anywhere during Mishmash, the team and I had differnt assigned spots on where we should film in the camp. We would go to the most populated areas in that sections and ask campers if they wanted to be in the Daily Video. We would help campers stage scenes of them doing cool activites, interacting with each other, and having an all around great time at camp. We took some many videos that we couldn't use. After an average week of filming, I would delete 3000 videos from my storage.
