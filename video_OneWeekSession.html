Camp Ozark: One Week Session Video


At Camp Ozark, campers attend sessions for one week. As a videographer, my biggest challenge was to capture all events of the week for all ages of campers, 5-17, and tell a cohesive story full of fun and memories for campers and their parents to share after the camp ended.

My Team

  • Me and 3 Other Videographers
  • 1 Video Editors
  • 1.5 hours editing

Time Taken

  • 6-7 hours filming
  • 5 days for editing

The Technology

  • Adobe Premiere Pro
  • Ronin S, Ronin M
  • Canon 80D
  • Drone
  • Wide Angle, Super Telephoto, and Super Telephoto Lenses

The Process

The rest of the video team and I spent the entire week filming and editing the One Week Session videos. At the end of the week, we showed the kids who stayed at the camp for one week the final cut and had DVDs for parents available to buy. To get to the final product, all the videographers shared film. One videographer would spend the rest of their time editing the video, while the others spent time editing the Daily Videos and the Team Hype Videos. The videographer in charge of editing the One Week Session videos was in change of making sure all the required film made it into the video was high quality and any missing sections were re-shot. The video is broken down into 6 sections:

  • Section 1: Opening Day - our goal was to show off campers arriving at camp, meeting their new friends and councelors, and checking out their living quarters for the week
  • Section 2 & 3: Days 2/3 & 4/5 - our goal was to capture all of the different events and activies campers could participate in
  • Section 4: Olympiad - our goal was to encapsulate the energy and team spirit that took place during the biggest relay race that took place through out the entire camp grounds
  • Section 5: Friends - our goal was to help campers reminisce on the friends they made at camp and say goodbye



When editing the film, we had to keep in mind the how the visuals would appear to campers and parents. We had to keep equity in mind and to make sure all of the campers appeared in the video, we made sure to show campers of all ages and genders in the video. We could only have at most 3 clips in a row that showed one gender or age. Clip that showed the same type of activity (water sports, shooting, crafts, or sports) also could not appear in a row.


One of the challenges of editing this video was that it was only for the campers who stay at camp for one week. These types of campers were also mixed in groups with kids who were staying for two or three weeks. In order to keep everyone and everything organized, the entire video team made sure to name our clips in the same style that included the age, gender, and length of stay of the campers in the video.
