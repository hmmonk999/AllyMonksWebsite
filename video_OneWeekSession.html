Camp Ozark: One Week Session Video


At Camp Ozark, campers attend sessions for one week. As a videographer, my biggest challenge was to capture all events of the week for all ages of campers, 5-17, and tell a cohesive story full of fun and memories for campers and their parents to share after the camp ended.

My Team

  • Me and 3 Other Videographers
  • 1 Video Editors
  • 1.5 hours editing

Time Taken

  • 6-7 hours filming
  • 5 days for editing

The Technology

  • Adobe Premiere Pro
  • Ronin S, Ronin M
  • Canon 80D
  • Drone
  • Wide Angle, Super Telephoto, and Super Telephoto Lenses

The Process

requirements: you try to only include 1 week campers

audience: kids watch the videos at the end, dvds for the parents (boss burns them)

goal: show the best moments at all of camp, reflect on the time they spent at camp, the frinds they made

filming: shoot manually, have adjust settings based on lighting,

follow a script: opening day (highlights of kids arriving at camps (busses and cars), moving in, meeting cousolors and new friends, team buildin), days 2/3 ( ), days 4/5 (), olympad (big relay race - race to each activity, you run along side them), friends (kids waving, but be creative)

event ratio of creative shots (crafts), adventure shots (ropes course), watershots, shooting sports, atheltics (sports), can't have any of these shots one after another, 3 of the same gender in a row

scuba diving shots

keeping up to date to see if you didn't get the right clip or need better footage

high light which videos need improvement, work with the team,

big stresser: adding motion to shots, add as much motion as possibe
